Buildmate Specialist Products Limited was founded by a Ghanaian family called Nuako, in Ghana. It was called Bizzel Enterprise in its early stages of business in 2008. The main objective of the business then, was importing paint from Thailand. However, Influenced by the nature of the construction industry in the country, the company saw an opportunity window to move into the trend of building construction material. The company realized that customers needed other things beyond the scope of paints, therefore decided to go into building chemicals like sealant, water proofing, add mixtures, bonding agents, special mortars amongst others.

 

 

In 2014 the companyâ€™s name was changed to Buildmate Specialist Product Limited. The company kept growing as a business, venturing into other aspects of construction such as plasters, ceilings and special mortars. In no time Buildmate saw other opportunities in decorations. Soon the company was dealing in special effects for buildings such as wall papers and special effect paints.

 

 

Since the expansion of the business, Buildmate has become the one stop shop for special building construction products. Although the company started as a paint brand, it is now the leading company in the sector of special chemicals for construction in Ghana. Buildmate partners with foreign suppliers such as Sherwin-Williams, Sika, SODA Building Services, Danosa amongst others.

 

With a supply chain of over 5000 different building product, Buildmate currently has products circulating all over the ten regions of Ghana. Aside importing other branded products from other foreign companies, Buildmate has its own in-house products such as SUPER CROWN; their flagship product, Europlaster, Europlaster boards, Europlaster p.o.p amongst others.
The company has endeavored to be a part of most major construction projects in Ghana, covering a wide scope of both private and governmental construction projects in the country. Buildmate is located at Tudu -Accra, Ghana and it is equipped with two warehouses; Amasaman on the Kumasi road and one at Achimota.
